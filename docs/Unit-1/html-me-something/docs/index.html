By Chris Russo - April 30, 2020

The Tampa Bay Buccaneers entered the 2020 NFL Draft with a few specific needs, which were addressed with their first three picks of the draft. This is my short take on each pick:

Round 1 - Tristan Wirfs - T

Regardless of QB Tom Brady turning 43 this year, even in his youth he was never known as a mobile quarterback, so it was imperative the Bucs got help on the line. They did so with Wirfs, who brings high athleticism and is able to play both sides of the offensive line.

Round 2 - Antoine Winfield, Jr. - S

After a poor start to the 2019 season, the defensive backfield finished strong. Still, it was regarded as one of the weak links on the team. This was addressed by adding safety Winfield, who is able to play several different formations and will fit nicely in defensive coordinator Todd Bowles' theme.

Round 3 - Ke'Shawn Vaughn - RB

The departure of Peyton Barber leaves Ronald Jones as the feature running back. Help was needed, and obtained, by taking Vaughn in the 3rd. Vaughn is a durable back who is also able to block for QB Brady. He'll be the perfect compliment to Jones' style.

Round 5 - Tyler Johnson - WR

Not having a 4th round pick, the Bucs take wide receiver Johnson in the 5th. He joins pro-bowlers Mike Evans and Chris Godwin, and will likely be #3 on the depth chart. Johnson will replace Breshad Perriman who exits in 2020, playing in the slot and on downs Evans or Godwin is resting.

Round 6 - Khalil Davis - DT

With a couple of star defensive lineman aging, it made sense to take Davis in this position. Although he won't start right away, he provides stability for the future and will be able to help keep the starters fresh by playing in rotation.

Round 7 - Chappelle Russell - LB

Although the Bucs have one of the most solid linebacking starting units in the league, they did have some problems with injuries this past season, leaving the position thin. Russell provides some much needed depth in case of future injury.

Round 7 - Raymond Calais - RB

With their second pick in the 7th round - and final pick of the draft for the Bucs - they select another running back in Calais. He is a smaller back with great speed and quickness, who can play a "slash" type role. He'll be expected to have a greater impact in the passing game.
